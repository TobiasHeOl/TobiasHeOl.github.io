Tobias Hegelund Olsen

I’m currently a PhD student in Immunoinformatics at the Oxford Protein Informatics Group (OPIG), University of Oxford. I’m funded via the EPSRC Sustainable Approaches to Biomedical Science Centre for Doctoral Training: Responsible and Reproducible Research (SABS:R3) program.

My research interests lie within studying immunological proteins (Antibodies, T-Cell Receptors, MHCs) using machine learning and other data science approaches. Currently, my focus is on analysing large antibody sequence datasets using state-of-the-art machine learning methods.

Created bioinformatic tools includes an antibody paratope predictor (proABC-2, article, web server), a peptide emulsifier predictor (EmulsiPred, article, code) and a peptide antioxidant predictor (AnOxPePred, article, web server). Additionally, I have also worked on TCR-pMHC interactions and de-immunogenization of proteins.
